Artificial Intelligence




We live in an age driven by the need for surveillance for security reasons, but it is not limited to just that. There is real world statistics that reveal keeping an eye out for questionable activities in corporate work environments has played a key role in increasing the overall productivity of the environments.

The age-old method of placing a man in a watchtower where he’s ringing bells at the oncoming hostiles, has evolved into modern surveillance systems. The man in the watchtower is still there, don’t jump the gun just yet, he is just much more overworked and his ability to distinguish between objects over a long distance is bound by inherent biological limits. He’s under fire from the ever fluctuating frame rate which rendered him slow.

As humans, we have always had a drive to make things faster, better, more efficient and most importantly turning them into unbiased machines that take us away from the equation to whatever extent possible, reducing manual-labor, and saving us time.

The artificial intelligence steps in to save the day here, wearing the cape of video and behavior analytics it taps with our sleepy watchtower guy and like a cocaine junky on the run from cops, essentially never stops or bows out in the ring, we might have a tag team champion on our hands soon.

Artificial intelligence based on video analysis works in the form of a questionnaire, it will see objects like a moving vehicle and it will ask itself questions. What are the heights, widths and other such characteristics of these objects? Then it compares them with hundreds of thousands of ‘reference’ images, if at the end of the examination the result of the questionnaire matches a predefined value (in a sense, a rule set by the user beforehand), the AI sends out an alert signal.

With every passing day these systems get more competent as they can gauge speed patterns. For instance, if an intruder is running into a property where any fast moving object was not seen before, the system notifies the user. Similarly distance and a variety of other parameters can be gauged. In a place where we have never learned to stay satisfied and have never stopped striving for better, the better was delivered.

A better system that doesn’t need a limit or a rule from the user, a system that can watch and learn by itself. This system studies an area and makes references based on behavior. For instance, a road while objects like a car are moving on it shouldn’t have smaller objects crossing it, say humans, if the system detects an anomaly as such, it triggers an alarm. These systems are called behavior analytic or non-rule based AI(s).

The only limitation they have is perhaps the low resolution they currently work on. An object like a human, more than sixty meters away will make things hard. This too is due to the sheer processing power they require. In not so distant future, we will have a workaround for that and will have an entirely new system in place because the world is open to possibilities.

With every day that passes, the surveillance systems are improved, we are removing the sloppy watchtower guy and his bull-horn, he will be given a better role. An AI system will sit in his space and consume significantly less caffeine will have better judgment as it will stop trespasses and will prevent fires and health hazards. Moreover, it will indulge human brain to work on better things( you should really reconsider scrolling through social media during those work hours now), false alarms will be reduced, limitations of human physical capabilities will be lifted.

To leave with a word for this future with confidence, at least this can be said that AI is here to help and it is here to save us time, effort and resources. Nevertheless, it is definitely here to stick around for a while.
